# Customer Interview: VPE at Northgate (2026-03-11)

## Summary

Interview with the head of engineering at Northgate covering board-prep workflow, current tool friction, and willingness to pay.

## Key Claims (from the interview)

- Board prep takes 4–6 hours per quarter, mostly assembling a coherent story from screenshots and spreadsheets.
- LinearB's dashboard is "great for me, bad for the board" — too dense, too many micro-metrics, no narrative.
- Self-serve setup matters: the VPE will not negotiate a sales cycle for a tool the team might churn after a quarter.
- Pricing per seat is a non-starter at their company size; workspace-based pricing is "the only model that fits how we make tooling decisions."
- Would pay $1k–$3k/mo for the right thing. Above that, it becomes an enterprise-procurement conversation.

## Notes

The "great for me, bad for the board" quote is a direct customer phrase worth preserving. Use it (anonymized) in marketing.
