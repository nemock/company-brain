# Disposable Pad as the Recurring-Revenue Engine

## Summary

The wearable (Vitalisens Cardio) is the capital good. The pad (Vitalisens Pad) is the consumable. Every commercial, regulatory, and operational decision should account for the pad as the long-term revenue and clinical-quality driver, not the wearable.

## Content

The strategic bet is that a clinically-cleared wearable plus a recurring pad business is more resilient and more defensible than a one-time hardware sale. This shapes:

- **Pricing**: pad pricing is the primary monetization surface. Wearable pricing is set to facilitate adoption, not maximize gross margin.
- **Clinical**: pad performance and adhesion characteristics drive the data-quality envelope. A pad failure is a monitoring failure.
- **Regulatory**: pad supply must be qualified, reproducible, and labeled in alignment with the wearable's IFU.
- **Channel**: distribution must support recurring fulfillment, not just initial hardware sale.

This pillar rules in:

- Capital-light wearable, recurring-revenue pad.
- Investment in pad manufacturing, qualification, and clinical performance.
- Pricing transparency on pad subscriptions.

This pillar rules out:

- A free-pad, premium-wearable inversion of the model.
- Any product variant that ships without a defined pad consumable.
