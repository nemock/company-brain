# Question: Do We Need a Sensitive-Skin Pad Formulation?

## Summary

We ship one pad SKU in v1. A sensitive-skin variant would expand patient coverage but adds regulatory, manufacturing, and inventory complexity. Is it worth it, and at what trigger?

## Why This Matters

- A variant pad has its own validation, labeling, and supply-chain implications.
- If a meaningful fraction of patients cannot tolerate the v1 pad, that's a clinical exclusion problem.
- The decision affects pricing, packaging, and clinician guidance.

## What Would Answer It

- Cohort data on the proportion of patients reporting irritation severe enough to interfere with monitoring.
- Pad chemistry research on hypoallergenic alternatives that maintain the 7-day adhesion target.
- Cost-of-goods comparison.
- A clinical advisor's view on whether one variant or two becomes the better v1.x product.
