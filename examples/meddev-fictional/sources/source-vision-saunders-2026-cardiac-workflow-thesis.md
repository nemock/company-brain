# Founder Vision: The Cardiac Workflow Bottleneck (Saunders, 2026)

## Summary

The founder's stated thesis: in ambulatory cardiac monitoring, the rate-limiting step is not signal quality or sensor performance. It is the workflow time between event and clinician action. Vitalisens optimizes for workflow time first; sensor specs second.

## Key Claims

- Modern MCT devices have largely solved the signal-acquisition problem.
- What remains is the clinician-side workflow: how quickly a clinically significant event becomes a documented finding the cardiologist can act on.
- Disposable-pad business models align incentives toward continuous monitoring at scale.
- Online-only documentation supports rapid iteration on workflow.

## Interpretation for the Business

This vision shapes:

- ICP (ambulatory, workflow-bottlenecked clinics).
- Pricing (subscription pad model).
- Documentation strategy (online-only).
- Feature priorities (clinician app workflow, not consumer features).
